the process

At the START of any branding project, I often ask my clients to answer some business-related questions and send examples of work they've seen and liked. This helps me get to know what they really want; it's crucial to start a visual dialogue early. Amy's inspiration pieces involved patterns and colours, a mixture of quirky and modern styling. You can see what she was speaking to me in her language — of photography.

client inspiration

Using the client Moodboard, I typically do two or three boards that take inspiration first. I spend a couple of days researching, scanning my favourite sites for inspiration, discuss, add, eliminate and ultimately sort them into a cohesive direction. I then send them to the client. A good moodboard is as much about each individual image as it is the whole overall look. The moodboards are an important phase. It’s where I listen and see and hear what a client is responding to — and sometimes, even more importantly, what they hate.

MOODBOARDs A B C

During the process, I start playing with typography, patterns and logo design. At the early stage, I create the first two boards. As you can see, with the first two, it wasn't quite there yet – it lacked some of the depth and dimension incorporated in the final work. The revised board is brighter and bolder; the client loved it!

finally

I knew I wanted to create a brand for Amy that highlighted and celebrated her photography skills and design personality. And FINALLY, the finished identity was a two-colour concept. The brand is visually clean, sharp and modern. The camera shutter mark ads interest and ties in nicely with photography. I designed three logos, the first being the Primary Logo used on Amy's website. The Secondary logo to be used on Facebook and an alternative for Instagram. I also included Brand Guidelines which help understand the visual guidelines, voice and personality.
